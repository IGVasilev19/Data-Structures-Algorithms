The Last Signal
---------------

It was half past eleven — or so the old clock on the wall suggested — when
Dr. Elara Voss finally found what she'd been searching for.

"This can't be right," she whispered, leaning closer to the monitor. The
readout flickered: 0.003 Hz... 0.003 Hz... steady as a heartbeat.

She grabbed her notebook (the battered one with "E.V. — DO NOT TOUCH" scrawled
on the cover) and flipped to page 47. Her formula was there, exactly as she'd
written it three years ago: f(t) = A * sin(2πft + φ). She hadn't been wrong.
Nobody had believed her, but she hadn't been wrong.

The room was silent except for the hum of equipment; even the rain outside
seemed to hold its breath. Elara typed a quick message to her colleague, Dr.
Osei: "Come to Lab 3 — NOW. Bring the backup drive. Don't tell Hargrove."

She hit send, then sat back in her chair.

What happened next would change everything. Or nothing. She wasn't sure which
outcome frightened her more.

[End of Chapter 1]
